Plant Viruses & eIF4E

How viruses hijack translation initiation factors for infection

Why Viruses Target eIF4E

Plant viruses have evolved sophisticated mechanisms to manipulate host translation machinery for successful infection. The eIF4E protein family serves as a critical host factor for many plant viruses.

1

VPg Protein

Viruses encode a genome-linked protein (VPg) that mimics the m7G cap structure, binding to eIF4E

2

Translation Hijacking

VPg-eIF4E interaction recruits the translation machinery to viral RNA instead of host mRNA

3

Resistance Evolution

Natural eIF4E polymorphisms can disrupt VPg binding while maintaining cap-binding function

Major Virus Families

Plant virus families known to interact with eIF4E proteins

Potyviridae

Most Studied

Examples: Tobacco etch virus (TEV), Potato virus Y (PVY), Turnip mosaic virus (TuMV)

Hosts: Wide range including Solanaceae, Brassicaceae, Fabaceae

eIF4E Interaction: VPg protein binds to eIF4E and eIF(iso)4E isoforms

Resistance: Multiple natural resistance alleles identified (e.g., pvr2 in pepper)

Comoviridae

Examples: Cowpea mosaic virus (CPMV), Bean pod mottle virus (BPMV)

Hosts: Primarily legumes (Fabaceae)

eIF4E Interaction: VPg-mediated, similar to potyviruses

Resistance: eIF4E alleles in pea and bean conferring resistance

Secoviridae

Examples: Rice tungro spherical virus (RTSV), Lettuce mosaic virus (LMV)

Hosts: Various including monocots and dicots

eIF4E Interaction: VPg-dependent translation initiation

Resistance: eIF4E-based resistance in lettuce varieties
